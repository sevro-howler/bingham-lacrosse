Resources for Bingham Youth Lacrosse coaches.

## Practice Structure

### Typical Practice (75 minutes)

| Time | Activity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| 0-10 min | Warm-up | Jogging, stretching, dynamic movements |
| 10-25 min | Stick skills | Passing, catching, ground balls |
| 25-45 min | Drills | See drill plans |
| 45-65 min | Scrimmage | Small-sided games |
| 65-75 min | Cool down | Stretching, review, announcements |

## Drill Plans

### Week 1-2: Fundamentals
- Ground ball technique
- Cradling and ball protection
- Basic passing and catching
- Defensive stance

### Week 3-4: Offensive Skills
- Dodging (split, roll, face)
- Shooting fundamentals
- Off-ball movement
- Fast breaks

### Week 5-6: Defensive Skills
- Body positioning
- Stick checks
- Sliding and help defense
- Clearing

### Week 7-8: Team Concepts
- Set offenses
- Zone defense
- Man-up/man-down
- Transition play

---

## Game Day Preparation

### Pre-Game (30 min before)
- Arrive early to set up
- Equipment check
- Warm-up laps
- Stick work
- Strategy review

### During Game
- Positive reinforcement
- Rotate players regularly
- Focus on effort over outcome
- Adjust strategies as needed

### Post-Game
- Cool down
- Team huddle
- Highlight positives
- Announce next practice/game

## Safety Guidelines

### Required Equipment
- [ ] Helmet with face mask
- [ ] Shoulder pads
- [ ] Elbow pads
- [ ] Gloves
- [ ] Mouthguard
- [ ] Athletic cup (recommended)

### Injury Protocol
1. Stop play immediately
2. Assess injury
3. Remove from play if necessary
4. Notify parents
5. Document incident

### Weather Guidelines
- **Lightning:** Clear field immediately, wait 30 min after last strike
- **Heavy rain:** Cancel if field becomes unsafe
- **Heat:** Increase water breaks, watch for exhaustion
