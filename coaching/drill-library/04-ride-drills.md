# Ride Drills

**Phase focus:** Teaching what riding means (pressuring the other team when they try to clear after we shoot)

---

## What is Riding? ⭐ *FOR THURSDAY*

**Phase:** Ride
**Objective:** Understand that riding means "chase the other team when they try to clear the ball"
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, 2-3 balls, cones, pinnies for two teams
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Goal at one end with a goalie (or coach)
- Defensive zone marked with cones (about 30x30 yards around goal)
- 3 offensive players (attackers)
- 3 defensive players (trying to clear)

### I Do (3 min) - Coach Demonstrates
**Show the concept:**
- "We just shot on their goal! Watch what happens when their goalie gets the ball..."
- Goalie picks up ball
- Coach (as attacker): "Now I RIDE - I chase them and try to get the ball back!"
- Demonstrate running at the clearing player: "RIDE! Pressure them!"
- "If we get the ball back, we can shoot again right away!"

**Key terms to emphasize:**
- **"RIDE!"** - Pressure the other team's clear
- **"Get it back!"** - Try to force a turnover

### We Do (6 min) - Guided Practice
**Walk through:**
1. Start with goalie/defender with ball
2. 3 attackers (offense) set up to ride
3. Coach: "Their goalie has it - what do we do?"
4. Players: "RIDE!"
5. Attackers chase and pressure the clear (start slow/half speed)
6. If they cause a turnover, celebrate!
7. If defense clears it successfully, restart
8. Repeat several times, rotating players

**Coaching points:**
- ONE main thing: Pressure them! Don't let them clear easily!
- "Run at them when they get the ball!"
- Keep it simple - just chase and pressure

### You Do (6 min) - Independent Practice
**Live riding:**
- Full speed 3v3 ride scenario
- Offense shoots, then immediately rides
- Defense tries to clear
- Play for 20-30 seconds each rep
- Multiple reps, rotate teams

**Success criteria:**
- Do they immediately turn and pressure after the shot?
- Understand riding = trying to get ball back quickly?

### Game Connection
"In games, after you shoot, don't just stand there! RIDE and try to steal the ball back for another shot!"

### Common Mistakes
- Walking back after a shot (remind: "RIDE! Chase them!")
- Not understanding when to ride (remind: "Right after we shoot!")

## No Easy Pickups

**Phase:** Ride
**Objective:** When the ball is on the ground, pressure the other team so they can't just pick it up easily
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 3-4 balls, cones for boundaries
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Small grid (15x15 yards)
- Pairs of players: 1 offense (trying to clear), 1 defense (riding)
- Ball on ground between them

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- Put ball on ground
- "If I let the other team just walk up and get it, that's an EASY PICKUP!"
- Show it: walk slowly, let opponent pick it up
- "That's bad! Watch this instead: NO EASY PICKUPS!"
- Sprint at the ball, pressure the opponent: "Now it's hard for them!"

**Key terms:**
- **"No Easy Pickups!"** - Pressure ground balls
- **"Pressure!"** - Don't let them get it free

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice together:**
1. Ball on ground between pairs
2. On whistle, both players sprint to ball
3. Offensive player tries to pick it up
4. Riding player tries to pressure (light contact, focus on presence)
5. Coach: "NO EASY PICKUPS! Make it hard!"
6. Switch roles, repeat many times

**Coaching points:**
- Sprint to every ground ball!
- Even if you don't get it, PRESSURE them
- Make them work for it

### You Do (6 min)
**Competitive ground balls:**
- Same drill, full intensity
- Keep score: 1 point for clean pickup, 0 if pressured
- Emphasize: even causing a bobble = success!

**Success criteria:**
- Do they sprint to pressure ground balls?
- Understand they can cause turnovers this way?

### Game Connection
"In games, ground balls are BIG! If you pressure them, they might drop it and YOU get the ball back!"

### Common Mistakes
- Jogging to ground balls (remind: "SPRINT! No easy pickups!")
- Giving up if opponent gets there first (remind: "Still pressure them!")

## Ride and Recover

**Phase:** Ride → Transition to Defense
**Objective:** Ride for a few seconds, then recover back to defense if they clear successfully
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, 2-3 balls, cones for midline
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Goal at one end
- Midline marked with cones (about 40 yards from goal)
- 3 attackers (riders), 3 defenders (clearing)

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "We ride for a few seconds to try to get the ball back..."
- Demonstrate riding pressure
- "...but if they clear it past us, we RECOVER and get back on defense!"
- Show running back: "Get back! Now we're on defense!"
- "Ride, but don't chase forever - recover!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Recover!"** - Get back to defense
- **"Get back!"** - Transition to defense

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice the sequence:**
1. Attackers shoot (miss into goalie)
2. Goalie picks up ball
3. Attackers RIDE for 5 seconds (coach counts)
4. After 5 seconds, coach yells "RECOVER!"
5. Attackers sprint back past midline
6. This teaches: ride, then transition back

**Coaching points:**
- Ride hard, but know when to get back
- Sprint back on recovery - don't jog!

### You Do (6 min)
**Live drill:**
- 3v3 ride scenario
- After 5 seconds OR if clear is successful, riders must recover
- If they don't get back fast enough, clearing team can score
- Teaches both ride AND transition to defense

**Success criteria:**
- Do they pressure on the ride?
- Do they recover quickly when it's time?

### Game Connection
"In games, if they clear it past you, GET BACK! Don't chase them all the way down - recover and defend!"

### Common Mistakes
- Chasing too long (remind: "Recover! Get back!")
- Jogging back instead of sprinting (remind: "Sprint back!")
