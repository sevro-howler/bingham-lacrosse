# Offense Drills

**Phase focus:** Teaching what offense means (trying to score, getting open, working as a team)

## Shooting Line ⭐ *FOR TONIGHT*

**Phase:** Offense
**Objective:** Learn to shoot from the shooting line (12-15 yards out), not run all the way to the crease
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1-2 goals, lots of balls, cones to mark the shooting line
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Place cones in an arc 12-15 yards from the goal (the "shooting line")
- Players line up behind the cones
- Pile of balls at the line
- Can run 2 lines if you have 2 goals

### I Do (3 min) - Coach Demonstrates
**Show the concept:**
- "In offense, we want to shoot from HERE (point to line), not run all the way in!"
- "Watch me: I get to the shooting line, and I shoot!"
- Demonstrate: catch/pickup ball, get to the line, shoot
- Then show the WRONG way: "Don't do this - run all the way to the crease and shoot"
- "See the difference? Shooting from the LINE gives us better shots!"

**Key terms to emphasize:**
- **"Shooting Line!"** - This is where we shoot from
- **"Don't run to the crease!"** - Stop at the line

### We Do (6 min) - Guided Practice
**Practice together:**
1. Each player gets a ball
2. Walk to the shooting line (slowly at first)
3. Stop at the line, set feet
4. Shoot on goal
5. Coach reinforces: "Stop at the line! Good!"
6. Repeat 3-4 times per player
7. Gradually add speed (dodge to the line, then shoot)

**Coaching points:**
- ONE main thing: STOP at the shooting line, don't keep running!
- Celebrate when they stop properly: "Perfect! You stopped at the line!"
- Form doesn't matter - just want them to STOP at the line

### You Do (6 min) - Independent Practice
**Shooting contest:**
- Players shoot from the line
- 1 point for hitting the goal from the line
- 0 points if they run past the line
- Makes it competitive and reinforces the concept!

**Success criteria:**
- Do they stop at the shooting line instead of running all the way in?
- Do they understand WHY (better angle, more time to shoot)?

### Game Connection
"In games, if you run all the way to the crease, the goalie can just step out and block you! Shooting from the LINE is smarter offense!"

### Common Mistakes
- Running past the line to the crease (stop the drill, have them reset)
- Shooting while running (remind: "Stop, set feet, THEN shoot!")

## Peanut Butter

**Phase:** Offense
**Objective:** Get open in a triangle formation around the goal (like spreading peanut butter)
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, 1 ball, pinnies for offense
**Group size:** 6-9 players (3v3 or 4v4)

### Setup
- Goal at one end
- 3 offensive players with pinnies
- 3 defensive players (or start without defense)
- Draw imaginary triangle around goal

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "In offense, we don't all stand in one spot! We spread out like PEANUT BUTTER on bread!"
- Show 3 positions around goal: "One here (left), one here (right), one here (top)"
- "See the triangle? We cover the whole area!"
- "This gives us space to catch and shoot!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Peanut Butter!"** - Spread out in a triangle
- **"Find Open Space!"** - Don't bunch up

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice positioning:**
1. Put 3 offensive players around the goal
2. Coach moves them into triangle positions: "You here, you here, you there"
3. "See the triangle? That's peanut butter!"
4. Add the ball: pass around the triangle
5. Practice: catch, pass to next point of triangle
6. Add a rule: can only shoot if you're at a triangle point

**Coaching points:**
- Stay spread out - don't bunch together
- If someone moves, adjust the triangle

### You Do (6 min)
**Small-sided game:**
- 3v2 or 3v3 keep-away
- Offense must maintain triangle shape
- Coach calls "PEANUT BUTTER!" if they bunch up
- Try to score while maintaining spread

**Success criteria:**
- Do they spread out around the goal?
- Can they maintain spacing?

### Game Connection
"In games, if everyone bunches up, the defense can guard all of you easily! Spread out like peanut butter and they can't cover everyone!"

### Common Mistakes
- All running to the ball (remind: "Spread out!")
- Standing in a line instead of triangle (coach reposition them)

## Soft Pass Keep-Away

**Phase:** Offense
**Objective:** Learn to pass softly and keep ball away from defense (basic offensive possession)
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 ball, cones for boundary, pinnies
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Square boundary with cones (20x20 yards)
- 3 offensive players with pinnies
- 2 defensive players
- 1 ball

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "In offense, we want to KEEP the ball and pass it around!"
- "Watch: SOFT PASS to my teammate, away from defense"
- Demonstrate a few passes: "Soft! Easy! Right to the hands!"
- "If we keep passing, defense gets tired and we can score!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Soft Pass!"** - Don't throw hard, make it easy to catch
- **"Keep Away!"** - Pass it before defense gets to you

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice together:**
1. Start 3v2 in the grid
2. Offense passes, trying to complete 5 passes in a row
3. Defense tries to intercept (not too hard at first)
4. Coach counts passes out loud: "One! Two! Soft pass! Three!"
5. If defense gets it, flip roles

**Coaching points:**
- Soft passes are easier to catch!
- Pass BEFORE defense gets to you
- Move to open space after passing

### You Do (6 min)
**Competitive:**
- 3v2 keep-away game
- Offense gets 1 point for every 5 passes
- Defense gets point for interceptions
- Rotate teams

**Success criteria:**
- Are they making soft, catchable passes?
- Do they understand this is basic offense (possession)?

### Game Connection
"In games, we can't always shoot right away! Keep the ball moving with soft passes until someone gets open for a good shot!"

### Common Mistakes
- Throwing too hard (remind: "Soft! Make it easy!")
- Standing still after passing (remind: "Move to space!")

## Downhill to Goal

**Phase:** Offense
**Objective:** When dodging, drive TOWARD the goal (downhill), not away from it
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1-2 goals, balls, cones
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Shooting line marked with cones
- Players start at top (about 20 yards out)
- Goal at the end

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "When we dodge in offense, we go DOWNHILL toward the goal!"
- "Watch me: I'm here (top), I dodge this way (toward goal posts) - DOWNHILL!"
- Show the WRONG way: "Not sideways, not backwards - DOWNHILL to the posts!"
- "Downhill = toward the goal!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Downhill!"** - Toward the goal/posts
- **"Step Off!"** - Simple power dodge move (step + go)

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice together:**
1. Players line up at top
2. Each player dodges downhill toward goal
3. Coach points the direction: "That way - downhill!"
4. Slow at first, then faster
5. Add shooting: dodge downhill, shoot at the line

**Coaching points:**
- Direction matters - always toward goal!
- Not about fancy moves, just go downhill

### You Do (6 min)
**Add a cone defender:**
- Place a cone as a "defender"
- Players dodge around it, going downhill
- Shoot after the dodge
- Focus: did they go toward goal?

**Success criteria:**
- Are they attacking the goal (downhill)?
- Not running sideways or away?

### Game Connection
"In games, the goal is THAT WAY! Always attack downhill toward the posts and you'll get better shots!"

### Common Mistakes
- Dodging sideways or away from goal (stop and redirect: "Downhill!")
- Fancy moves that go nowhere (remind: "Simple and downhill!")
