# Faceoff Drills

**Phase focus:** Teaching what a faceoff is and why it matters

---

## Man-Ball Faceoff ⭐ *FOR TONIGHT*

**Phase:** Faceoff
**Objective:** Understand what a faceoff is and learn the "Man-Ball" concept (who gets the ball, who guards who)
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 3-4 balls, 2 cones (for faceoff X), pinnies for two teams
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Place 2 cones about 3 feet apart (the faceoff "X")
- Split players into two teams with pinnies
- Players line up on opposite sides

### I Do (3 min) - Coach Demonstrates
**Show the concept:**
- "A faceoff happens at the start of each quarter and after every goal!"
- "Two players fight for the ball here (point to X)"
- "Watch me: I'm going for the BALL. My teammates watch for the MAN."
- Demonstrate getting the ball, then pointing: "I got ball! You guard that man!"

**Key terms to emphasize:**
- **"Man-Ball"** - Who's got the ball? Who's guarding who?
- **"Ball!"** - Yell it when you get it

### We Do (6 min) - Guided Practice
**Walk through together:**
1. Put 2 players at the X with a ball between them
2. "On my whistle, try to get the ball - just get control and pick it up!"
3. Blow whistle, players go for ball
4. When one gets it, STOP the drill: "Who has ball? Who has man?"
5. Repeat with different pairs - coach calling out "MAN-BALL!" each time

**Coaching points:**
- ONE main thing: Identify who got the ball vs who needs to guard
- "After faceoff, we either have BALL or we need to guard MAN!"
- Keep it slow and clear

### You Do (6 min) - Independent Practice
**Let them try:**
- Continuous faceoffs with pairs
- After each faceoff, players identify: "I got ball!" or "I got man!"
- Rotate through so everyone gets 2-3 faceoff attempts
- Keep energy high, make it competitive!

**Success criteria:**
- Do kids understand that faceoff = fight for the ball to start?
- Can they identify ball vs man after the faceoff?

### Game Connection
"In a game, every quarter and after every goal, we do a faceoff! If you win the faceoff, we get to attack. If they win it, we have to defend!"

### Common Mistakes
- Trying to be too technical (just get the ball any way you can!)
- Confusion after the faceoff about who does what (keep reinforcing "Man-Ball")

## Wing Help

**Phase:** Faceoff
**Objective:** Learn that wings help the faceoff player get the ball
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 ball, 4 cones, pinnies
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Faceoff X in middle (2 cones)
- 2 cones on wings, about 10 yards to each side
- 1 faceoff player per team, 2 wing players per team

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "The faceoff player tries to get the ball, but they need HELP!"
- "Watch the wings: they run in to help as soon as the whistle blows!"
- Demonstrate: blow whistle, wings sprint to the ball to support
- "See? We help our teammate get the ball!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Help!"** - Wings help the faceoff guy
- **"Support!"** - Be ready to grab a loose ball

### We Do (6 min)
**Walk through:**
1. Set up: 1 faceoff + 2 wings per team
2. Ball on the ground at X
3. Whistle blows, faceoff players + wings all go for ball
4. First team to get clean possession wins
5. Rotate players through positions

**Coaching points:**
- Wings: run to the ball immediately, don't wait!
- Support your teammate getting the ball

### You Do (6 min)
**Independent practice:**
- Full faceoff setup with wings
- Competitive reps
- Celebrate when wings help secure the ball!

**Success criteria:**
- Do wings run to help right away?
- Do they understand they're supporting the faceoff player?

### Game Connection
"In games, the faceoff is 3v3 - one in the middle, two wings helping! We want ALL THREE working together!"

### Common Mistakes
- Wings standing still (remind: "Run to help!")
- Not understanding their job is to support

## Faceoff to Fast Break

**Phase:** Faceoff → Transition
**Objective:** Connect faceoff to the next phase (transition offense)
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 2 balls, cones for faceoff X and far cone
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Faceoff X at midfield
- Cone at offensive end (goal area)
- Two teams

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the connection:**
- "Watch: we win the faceoff, then what happens?"
- Demonstrate: get ball at faceoff → yell "WHEELS!" → sprint to offensive cone
- "Faceoff gets us the ball, then we TRANSITION to offense!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Faceoff to offense!"** - Link the phases
- **"WHEELS!"** - Run fast after we get it

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice the sequence:**
1. Faceoff at X
2. Winner grabs ball and sprints to offensive cone
3. Losing team doesn't defend (just learning the sequence)
4. Repeat, rotate players

**Coaching points:**
- As soon as you get the ball, think OFFENSE!
- Connect the phases in their mind

### You Do (6 min)
**Full speed:**
- Competitive faceoffs
- Winner sprints to score on goal
- Loser tries to catch them (intro to transition defense)

**Success criteria:**
- Do they understand faceoff leads to offense?

### Game Connection
"Win the faceoff, score quick! That's how games are won!"

### Common Mistakes
- Celebrating the faceoff win instead of transitioning (remind: "Keep going!")
