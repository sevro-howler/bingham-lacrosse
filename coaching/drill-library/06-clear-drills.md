# Clear Drills

**Phase focus:** Teaching what clearing means (getting the ball out of our defensive end and up the field)

---

## Clear It Fast ⭐ *FOR THURSDAY*

**Phase:** Clear
**Objective:** After getting the ball on defense, throw it up field quickly — don't hold it, don't cock the stick
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, 4-5 balls, 4 cones (box shape ~30x40 yards)
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Goal at one end (our goal we're defending)
- 4 cones making a box: 2 near the goal (defensive corners), 2 at midfield (outlets)
- 2-3 players near goal (defenders/goalie clearing)
- 2-3 players at midfield cones (outlet receivers)

### I Do (3 min) - Coach Demonstrates
**Show the concept:**
- "We just made a save or got a ground ball on defense. Now what?"
- "We CLEAR — get the ball OUT of our end and up the field!"
- Demo: pick up ball near goal, look up field, throw to outlet at midfield
- "See? CLEAR means throw it! Don't hold it, don't run with it, just CLEAR!"
- Show the WRONG way: pick up ball, hold it, run around near goal
- "That's bad — the other team will take it and score!"

**Key terms to emphasize:**
- **"Clear!"** - Throw the ball up field
- **"No cocking!"** - Don't wind up big, just get rid of it

### We Do (6 min) - Guided Practice
**Walk through:**
1. Coach/goalie starts with ball at the goal
2. Outlet players stand at midfield cones with sticks up as targets
3. Coach: "I got the ball on defense — what do I do?"
4. Players: "CLEAR!"
5. Coach throws to outlet: "Clear! Gone!"
6. Now players take turns: pick up ball near goal, find the outlet, throw it
7. Emphasize: quick release, no cocking the stick way back
8. Walk speed first, then add pressure

**Coaching points:**
- ONE main thing: Get rid of the ball FAST
- Look up, find the open player, throw it
- "No cocking!" — short quick throw, not a windup

### You Do (6 min) - Independent Practice
**Clearing relay:**
- Split into 2 teams
- Ball starts at goal, player clears to midfield outlet
- Outlet catches, sprints to other end
- Time each team — fastest clear wins!
- Emphasize quick release at the goal end

**Success criteria:**
- Do they get rid of the ball quickly?
- Are they throwing without a big windup?
- Do they look for the outlet player?

### Game Connection
"In games, after our goalie makes a save, we CLEAR the ball fast! If we hold it too long, the other team steals it and scores on us!"

### Common Mistakes
- Holding the ball too long near our goal (remind: "CLEAR! Get rid of it!")
- Winding up to throw (remind: "No cocking! Quick throw!")
- Not looking for the outlet (remind: "Look up! Find your teammate!")

## Find Space to Clear

**Phase:** Clear
**Objective:** Move to open space so the goalie/defender can pass to you during a clear
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, 2-3 balls, cones, pinnies
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Goal at one end
- 3 clearing players spread around defensive end
- 1 player with ball at goal (goalie role)
- Optional: 1-2 riders adding light pressure

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the concept:**
- "When our goalie has the ball, he needs someone to throw to!"
- "But if you're standing behind a defender, he CAN'T see you!"
- Demo: stand behind someone — "Can the goalie throw to me? NO!"
- Move to open space — "Now? YES! I'm in the open! FIND OPEN SPACE!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Find open space!"** - Move where the goalie can see you
- **"Get open!"** - Don't hide behind defenders

### We Do (6 min)
**Practice movement:**
1. Goalie/coach has ball at goal
2. 3 players start bunched together near goal
3. Coach: "I can't throw to you — you're all bunched up! FIND SPACE!"
4. Players spread out to different areas
5. Coach throws to whoever is most open
6. Reset and repeat — players learn to spread out
7. Add 1 rider: now they have to move AWAY from pressure

**Coaching points:**
- Spread out — don't all go to the same spot!
- Show your stick as a target
- Move to where the goalie can see you

### You Do (6 min)
**Live clearing:**
- Goalie has ball, 3 clearers vs 1-2 riders
- Clearers must find space to receive the pass
- Successfully getting ball past midfield = point
- Rotate roles

**Success criteria:**
- Are they spreading out?
- Moving to open space?
- Showing their sticks as targets?

### Game Connection
"In games, if everyone stands in one spot, the other team covers you easy! FIND SPACE so we can clear the ball!"

### Common Mistakes
- All running to the same spot (remind: "Spread out!")
- Standing still (remind: "Move! Find space!")
- Not showing stick as target (remind: "Stick up! Show me where to throw!")

## Breakout Pattern

**Phase:** Clear
**Objective:** Learn a simple 3-player clearing pattern to get the ball out organized
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Equipment:** 1 goal, balls, 6 cones
**Group size:** 8-12 players

### Setup
- Goal at one end
- 3 cone positions: Left side, Right side, Middle (near midfield)
- Goalie at goal with ball
- 3 clearing players at cone positions

### I Do (3 min)
**Show the pattern:**
- "This is our BREAKOUT — our plan to clear the ball every time!"
- Draw it simple: Goalie → Left or Right → Middle → gone!
- Demo: Goalie passes to Left, Left passes to Middle, Middle runs it up
- "See? 1-2-3, ball is out! That's our breakout!"

**Key terms:**
- **"Breakout!"** - Call it to start the clearing pattern
- **"1-2-3!"** - Goalie to side to middle

### We Do (6 min)
**Walk the pattern:**
1. Goalie starts with ball
2. Coach calls "BREAKOUT!"
3. Left player runs to space, goalie throws to Left
4. Middle player moves up, Left throws to Middle
5. Middle runs it past midfield — CLEAR!
6. Rotate positions so everyone learns each spot
7. Walk speed first, then jog, then run

**Coaching points:**
- Know your spot in the pattern
- Move BEFORE the ball comes to you
- Quick passes — no holding!

### You Do (6 min)
**Full speed breakout:**
- Run the pattern at game speed
- Add 1-2 riders for pressure
- Can the clearing team execute the breakout under pressure?
- Celebrate successful clears!

**Success criteria:**
- Do they know the pattern?
- Can they execute it without coach calling every step?

### Game Connection
"In games, when the ref blows the whistle after a save, we yell BREAKOUT and everyone goes to their spot! The other team won't know what hit them!"

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting their position (remind: "Where's your spot? Go!")
- Holding the ball too long in the pattern (remind: "Quick! 1-2-3!")
